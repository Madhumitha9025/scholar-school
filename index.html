About Us

Vision

The vision at Scholar School is to prepare and motivate our students for a rapidly changing world by inculcating in them critical thinking skills, a global perspective, and a respect for core values of honesty, loyalty, perseverance, and compassion. Students will have success for today and be prepared for tomorrow.

Goals

Scholar School strives to give each child a lifelong appreciation of learning. Almost all the students in our school are from very poor and broken families dwelling in rural areas. Our sole aim is to provide them with a rich educational environment that can enable every student with a broad foundation of knowledge and practical experience. The curriculum integrates with our Tamil Nadu Government Standards and is delivered through multi-graded classroom programs. Well-rounded instruction is complemented by a variety of programs, including yoga, physical education, as well as visual, performing, and culinary arts. Students develop a sense of community while maintaining individuality and creative self-expression. Our diverse community fosters cooperative skills, mutual respect, and pride in academic progress.

Our Curriculum

The Pre Primary Program for Nursery to Class II offers a stimulating, caring and relaxed atmosphere of hands on experiential learning to develop skills that set them on the road to be great learners. Work and play are inter-woven to help children explore their curiosity and make every moment a learning experience.

Language

A young mind is always amazed by oral language and stories with expression. In our school we create a rich educational environment that enables children always interested and joyful in repeating vocabulary words. The students work with many different pictures, cards and books to establish their vocabulary. They are also encouraged do express their feelings freely. Scholar school provides the Montessori classroom; in which, a child learns the phonetic sounds of the letters before he learns the alphabetical names in a sequence. The phonetic sounds are given first because these are the sounds he hears in words that he needs to be able to read. The child first becomes aware of these phonetic sounds when the teacher introduces the consonants with sandpaper letters. The individual presentation of language materials in a Montessori classroom allows the teacher to take the advantage of each child's greatest periods of interest. Reading instructions begins on the day when the child wants to know what a word says or when he shows an interest in using the movable letters nearly always precedes reading in a Montessori environment. By doping many reading exercise which offer variety rather than monotonous repetition, gradually the child learns irregular words, and words with two and three syllables. Also available in the Montessori classroom are many attractive books which use a large number of phonetic words. Proceeding at their own pace, the child is encouraged to read about things which interest him. His skills in phonics give him the means of attacking almost any news word, so that he is not limited to specific number of words which he has been trained to recognize by sight. The child's interest in reading is never stifled by monotony. Rather, it is cultivated as his most important hey to future learning. He is encouraged to explore books for answers to his own questions.

Mathematics

It is our strong belief that if a child has access to mathematical equipment in his early years, he can easily and joyfully assimilate many facts and skills of arithmetic. On the other hand, these same facts and skills may require long hours of drill if they are introduced to them later in the abstract from. After observing that a child who becomes interested in counting likes to touch or move the items as he enumerates them, we follow Dr. Montessori who designed concrete materials to represent all types of quantities. By combining this equipment, separating it, sharing it, counting it, and comparing it, the child can demonstrate to himself the basic operation of mathematics. The child in our class does not sit down to memorize addition and subtraction facts. Rather, he learns these facts by actually performing the operations with concrete materials. When the child wants to do arithmetic, he is given a sheet of paper containing simple problems. He works the problems with appropriate materials and records his results. Similar operations can be performed with a variety of materials. These varieties maintain the child interest while giving him many opportunities for necessary repetition. As he commits the additional facts and the multiplications tables to memory, he gains a real understanding of what each operation means. They are many materials that can be used for numeration, addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Science and nature

Our school aims at inducing natural curiosity through the scientific studies such as discovery projects and experiments, which enable the child, draw his own conclusions. The plant and animal kingdoms re studied in an orderly fashion to foster a love and appreciation for all living things.

Geography

Among the most popular activities in the classroom, our school provides the large wooden maps. At first the child uses the maps simply as puzzles. Gradually he learns the names of many of the countries as well as information about culture, climate, and landmarks. The maps illustrate many geographical facts concretely. The child also learns about common land and water formations such as islands peninsulas, isthmus, gulfs and straits.
